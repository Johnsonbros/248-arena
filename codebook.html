248 CMR 2.00 — Definitions ▼

Key Definitions

  • Air Gap: The unobstructed vertical distance through free atmosphere between the lowest opening from any pipe or faucet supplying water to a tank, fixture, or other device and the flood-level rim of the receptacle.
  • Backflow: The flow of water or other liquids, mixtures, or substances into the distribution pipes of a potable supply of water from any source other than the intended source.
  • Branch: Any part of the piping system other than a main, riser, or stack.
  • Building Drain: The lowest piping that receives discharge from soil, waste, and other drainage pipes inside the building and conveys it to the building sewer.
  • Building Sewer: The part of the horizontal piping of a drainage system extending from the building drain to its connection with the main sewer or other point of disposal.
  • Cross-Connection: Any physical connection or arrangement between two otherwise separate piping systems, one of which contains potable water and the other water of unknown or questionable safety.
  • Fixture Unit (DFU): A quantity in terms of which the load-producing effects on the plumbing system of different kinds of plumbing fixtures are expressed on some arbitrarily chosen scale.
  • Flood-Level Rim: The edge of the receptacle from which water overflows.
  • Potable Water: Water that is safe and satisfactory for drinking, cooking, and bathing — free from impurities in amounts sufficient to cause disease or harmful physiological effects.
  • Rough-In: The installation of all parts of the plumbing system which can be completed prior to the installation of fixtures, including drainage, water supply, and vent piping.
  • Trap: A fitting or device designed to provide a liquid seal to prevent the back passage of air (sewer gas) without materially affecting the flow of sewage through it.
  • Vent System: A pipe or pipes installed to provide air circulation to or from a drainage system and to protect trap seals from siphonage and back pressure.
  • Water Service Pipe: The pipe from the water main or other source of water supply to the building served.
  • Wet Vent: A vent that also serves as a drain.
248 CMR 3.00 — General Regulations ▼

Administration

  • The Board of State Examiners of Plumbers and Gas Fitters administers 248 CMR
  • Only a licensed Master Plumber can obtain a plumbing permit
  • A plumbing permit is valid for 1 year from date of issuance
  • Rough-in plumbing must be inspected before concealment of piping

Licensing Requirements

  • Journeyman: Minimum 3 years practical experience under a licensed Master Plumber
  • A Journeyman must work under the general supervision of a Master Plumber
  • An Apprentice must work under the direct supervision of a Journeyman or Master Plumber

Permits

  • A plumbing permit IS required for: installing new fixtures, water heaters, water piping, gas piping
  • A plumbing permit is NOT required for: replacing washers, flappers, clearing drain stoppages
  • All plumbing work must conform to 248 CMR and is subject to inspection

Water Service

  • Minimum water service pipe size: 3/4 inch
  • Minimum burial depth for water service in MA: 48 inches (4 feet) to protect against freezing
  • A full-port shutoff valve (gate or ball valve) required at building entrance

248 CMR 5.00 — Water Distribution ▼

Pressure Requirements

  • Maximum static water pressure at any fixture: 80 psi
  • A pressure reducing valve (PRV) is required when pressure exceeds 80 psi
  • Minimum hot water temperature at fixtures (residential): 110°F
  • Water hammer arrestors required when quick-closing valves are installed (dishwashers, washing machines)

Thermal Expansion

  • A thermal expansion tank is required when a closed system exists (backflow preventer on water service)
  • The expansion tank absorbs pressure from heated water that cannot expand back into the main

Materials

  • Only lead-free solder (≤0.2% lead) on potable water systems
  • Copper tube types: K (green, heaviest — underground), L (blue — general), M (red — light duty), DWV (yellow)
  • Type K required for underground water service

Fitting Rules

  • A sanitary tee shall NOT be used to change direction horizontal to horizontal
  • A combination wye and 1/8 bend can replace a sanitary tee (horizontal to vertical)
  • Long-sweep 90° permitted for horizontal to horizontal changes

Cleanouts

  • Required at every change of direction greater than 45 degrees
  • Maximum spacing: 75 feet on horizontal drains
  • Cleanout size: same as pipe (up to 4"), then 4" for larger pipes
  • Cleanouts must be accessible

Venting

  • Purpose: equalize pressure, prevent trap siphonage and back pressure
  • Minimum vent size for a water closet: 2 inches
  • Minimum vent stack size: 2 inches (regardless of building drain size)
  • Vent through roof must extend minimum 12 inches above roof surface
  • Wet vent: serves as both a vent and drain for another fixture
  • Island vent (loop vent): typically used for kitchen sinks where conventional venting is impractical

248 CMR 7.00 — Gas Piping ▼

General Rules

  • Minimum gas pipe size from meter to appliance: 1/2 inch
  • Maximum pressure drop (low-pressure system, under 2 psi): 0.5 inch water column
  • A sediment trap (drip leg) is required at the inlet of every gas appliance
  • A shutoff valve is required at the meter and within 6 feet of each appliance
  • Only thread compounds approved for gas service shall be used

Testing

  • New gas piping test: 3 psi gauge pressure for minimum 10 minutes with no perceptible drop
  • Test medium: air or inert gas (never test with fuel gas)
  • All connections capped during test

Materials & Joints

  • Black iron (steel) pipe: Joined by threaded fittings or welding
  • CSST (corrugated stainless steel tubing): Approved with proper bonding
  • Copper tubing allowed for gas in some jurisdictions with approved fittings
  • Galvanized pipe is NOT permitted for gas downstream of the meter

248 CMR 8.00 — Private Sewage Disposal ▼

Septic Systems

  • Private sewage disposal required when public sewer is not available
  • Septic tank must be watertight and provide adequate settling/digestion
  • Minimum septic tank size for residential: typically 1,000 gallons for up to 3 bedrooms
  • Leaching field design based on soil percolation test results
  • Minimum setbacks from wells, property lines, and water courses are required

Key Requirements

  • Septic systems require a Title 5 (310 CMR 15.00) inspection for property transfer
  • Grease traps required for commercial food service before septic
  • No storm water shall be discharged to the septic system
248 CMR 10.00 — Plumber Licensing ▼

License Types

  • Apprentice: Works under direct supervision of a Journeyman or Master. Must register with the Board.
  • Journeyman: Minimum 3 years experience under a Master. Must pass written exam. Works under general supervision of a Master.
  • Master: Minimum 1 year as Journeyman (or equivalent). Must pass Master exam. Can pull permits, run a plumbing business, supervise Journeymen and Apprentices.

Exam Information

  • The Journeyman exam tests knowledge of 248 CMR — the Massachusetts State Plumbing Code
  • Exam is administered by the Board of State Examiners of Plumbers and Gas Fitters
  • Open-book format (248 CMR codebook allowed)
  • Passing score: 70%
  • Topics: DWV sizing, venting, water distribution, gas piping, backflow prevention, fixtures, materials, permits

Backflow Prevention (Critical Exam Topic)

  • Air Gap: Simplest and most reliable — physical separation between outlet and flood-level rim
  • Minimum air gap: 1 inch or 2x the outlet diameter, whichever is greater
  • RPZ (Reduced Pressure Zone): Required for high-hazard cross-connections (boiler makeup, chemical feed)
  • AVB (Atmospheric Vacuum Breaker): Must be installed 6 inches minimum above highest downstream outlet
  • RPZ assemblies must be tested annually by a certified tester
  • High hazard: Chemical feed systems, medical equipment, fire suppression with additives

Medical Gas (Exam Topic)

  • Installation by ASSE 6010 certified personnel only
  • Requires Type L copper (or K) cleaned and capped for oxygen service (ASTM B819)
  • Joints: brazed with BCuP alloy under continuous nitrogen purge
  • Soldering is NOT permitted on medical gas piping
